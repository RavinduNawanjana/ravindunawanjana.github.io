    <h1>Ravindu Nawanjana</h1>
    <p>
      I am an MSc student in International Banking and Finance at the Guildhall School of
      Business and Law, London Metropolitan University, where I also completed my BSc (Hons)
      in Banking and Finance. My research examines how financial institutions and market
      infrastructure &mdash; stock exchanges, multilateral development banks (MDBs), central
      banks, and private capital &mdash; can be structured, regulated, and incentivised to
      close the global climate finance gap, with a particular focus on emerging markets, the
      G77, and Small Island Developing States. I use mixed-methods research, combining
      large-scale practitioner surveys with semi-structured interviews of senior stakeholders
      at institutions including the World Bank and multilateral development banks, alongside
      comparative case analysis of stock exchanges and green finance instruments worldwide, to
      translate empirical findings into actionable policy frameworks for regulators, exchanges,
      and multilateral institutions.
    </p>
    <p>
      Alongside my academic research, I work at the intersection of climate policy and finance
      as Country Representative for the UN Food and Agriculture Organization&rsquo;s World Food
      Forum and for the Commonwealth&rsquo;s Sustainable Energy Transition programme, and as
      Head of Impact Investments and Sustainability at Kangara Holdings in Sri Lanka. I have
      represented my research and policy work at UNFCCC COP28 (Dubai), COP29 (Baku) and COP30
      (Bel&eacute;m), and UNCCD COP16 (Riyadh).
    </p>
