    <p>
      I am an MSc student in International Banking and Finance at the Guildhall School of
      Business and Law, London Metropolitan University, where I also completed my BSc (Hons)
      in Banking and Finance. My research examines how financial institutions and market
      infrastructure - stock exchanges, multilateral development banks (MDBs), central banks,
      and private capital - can be structured, regulated, and incentivised to close the global
      climate finance gap, with a particular focus on emerging markets, the G77, and Small
      Island Developing States. I use mixed-methods research, combining large-scale
      practitioner surveys with semi-structured interviews of senior stakeholders at
      institutions including the World Bank and multilateral development banks, alongside
      comparative case analysis of stock exchanges and green finance instruments worldwide, to
      translate empirical findings into actionable policy frameworks for regulators, exchanges,
      and multilateral institutions.
    </p>

## Research

<div class="paper-block">
  <h3><em>The Role of Stock Exchanges in Promoting the Banking Sector in Adopting Green Finance: A Global Analysis with Insights from Emerging Markets</em></h3>
  <p class="status">Published, December 2025 - ISSN 3051-5874</p>
  <p><a href="https://www.kandyconference.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/ICSBE-2025-Proceeedings-Book-Volume-II.pdf#page=522" target="_blank" rel="noopener">[Link to publication]</a> &nbsp;|&nbsp; <a class="cite-link" href="{{ '/assets/cite/stock-exchanges-citation.txt' | relative_url }}" target="_blank">Cite this paper</a></p>

  <p>
    This study investigates how stock exchanges can promote green finance adoption among banks
    globally, combining a synthesis of 64 sources with primary survey data from financial
    practitioners across emerging markets including India, Malaysia, Ghana, and Sri Lanka. Four
    interdependent drivers of adoption are identified - market regulations, market mechanisms,
    awareness, and incentives. Notably, IMF/IOSCO data show that only 49% of regulators in
    low-income economies maintain supportive market regulations, compared with 85% in
    high-income OECD nations. The findings indicate that stock exchanges must transition from
    voluntary guidance to binding frameworks mandating ESG disclosure and green bond standards
    for listed banks, supported by targeted tax incentives, formalised peer exchange
    partnerships, and standardised carbon market disclosure.
  </p>

<div class="paper-block">
  <h3><em>The Role of Multilateral Climate Funds in Shaping the Architecture of Climate Finance: An Analysis of Blended Finance and Institutional Collaboration among MDBs, Central Banks, and Private Capital across the G77 and China</em></h3>
  <p class="status">Working paper - expected June 2026</p>

  <p>
    This study examines the role of Multilateral Climate Funds (MCFs) in shaping climate
    finance architecture across the G77 and China, focusing on four determinants: institutional
    collaboration, blended finance deployment, policy and regulatory alignment, and
    transparency. I integrate quantitative data from 103 sustainable finance specialists with
    qualitative insights from eight semi-structured interviews with representatives of MDBs,
    central banks, and private capital institutions. Blended finance mechanisms emerge as the
    strongest predictor of climate finance architecture, acting as the primary catalyst for
    mobilising private capital - though their effectiveness is constrained by institutional
    fragmentation, bureaucratic silos between vertical funds and MDBs, and regulatory dissonance
    across financial systems. The paper introduces the Institutional Reinforcement Cycle, a
    recursive model theorising the interdependence of institutional, financial, and regulatory
    variables in determining climate finance effectiveness, and provides policy recommendations
    for evolving MCFs and MDBs beyond ODA and bilateral co-financing towards coordinated
    platforms that de-risk private investment at scale.
  </p>
</div>
